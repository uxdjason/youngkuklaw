## Case Facts

May Donoghue went to a café in Paisley where a friend purchased a bottle of ginger beer for her. The bottle was opaque, so she could not see the contents. After drinking some of the ginger beer, she poured the remainder into her glass, whereupon a decomposed snail floated out. Donoghue suffered gastroenteritis and nervous shock. She could not sue the manufacturer under contract because she did not buy the bottle, so she sued David Stevenson, the manufacturer, in tort.

## Held

The House of Lords held by a majority that the manufacturer owed a duty of care to the consumer. A manufacturer of products, which he sells in such a form as to show that he intends them to reach the ultimate consumer in the form in which they left him with no reasonable possibility of intermediate examination, owes a duty to the consumer to take reasonable care.

## Ratio Decidendi

1. You must take reasonable care to avoid acts or omissions which you can reasonably foresee would be likely to injure your neighbor.
2. A "neighbor" in law is any person so closely and directly affected by my act that I ought reasonably to have them in contemplation when directing my mind to the acts or omissions in question.
3. A manufacturer owes a duty of care to the ultimate consumer of their products.

## Obiter Dicta

Lord Atkin discussed the moral rule that you must love your neighbor, explaining how this is translated into the legal requirement that you must not injure your neighbor.
